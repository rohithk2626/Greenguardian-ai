Treatment Plan

1

Remove infected foliage

Immediately prune and bag all visibly infected leaves. Do not compost — dispose of in sealed bags to prevent spore spread.

2

Improve air circulation

Stake plants to keep leaves off the ground. Thin dense areas to allow air flow and faster drying after rain or irrigation.

3

Adjust watering habits

Switch to drip irrigation or base watering. Avoid wetting leaves — water early morning so foliage dries during the day.

1

Apply Chlorothalonil fungicide

Spray every 7–10 days as preventative and curative measure. Cover upper and lower leaf surfaces thoroughly.

2

Mancozeb (contact fungicide)

Apply 2g per liter of water. Effective against Alternaria species. Rotate with other fungicides to prevent resistance.

1

Copper-based spray

Apply copper hydroxide or copper sulfate solution. Safe for organic farming, effective at reducing fungal load.

2

Neem oil spray

Mix 2 tbsp neem oil + 1 tsp dish soap per liter water. Spray every 5–7 days in early morning or evening.

1

Crop rotation

Rotate tomatoes with non-solanaceous crops for at least 2–3 seasons to break the pathogen cycle in soil.

2

Resistant cultivars

Choose blight-resistant tomato varieties like Mountain Supreme or Defiant PhR for future planting.
